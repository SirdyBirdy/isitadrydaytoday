Is Today a Dry Day?

A dry day is a government-mandated day when the sale of alcohol is completely prohibited. In India, dry days are observed on national holidays — Republic Day (Jan 26), Independence Day (Aug 15), and Gandhi Jayanti (Oct 2) — plus additional state-specific observances. States like Gujarat, Bihar, Nagaland, Mizoram, and Manipur enforce year-round prohibition.

Across the Middle East, dry days coincide with Ramadan and Islamic holidays. Pakistan, Bangladesh, and the Maldives enforce near-total prohibition. In Southeast Asia, Thailand restricts alcohol on Buddhist holidays. Even in parts of the US, dry county laws persist.
