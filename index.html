Is Today a Bank Holiday in India?

A bank holiday in India is an officially gazetted day when all scheduled commercial banks remain closed, as mandated by the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) or respective state governments. India observes two categories: national holidays uniform across all states, and state-specific holidays that vary by region.

National Bank Holidays in India 2026

Three mandatory national bank holidays apply every year. Republic Day (January 26) commemorates the enactment of the Indian Constitution in 1950. Independence Day (August 15) marks the end of British colonial rule. Gandhi Jayanti (October 2) honours Mahatma Gandhi's birth anniversary.

Bank Holidays by City — Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore

Mumbai and Maharashtra observe Gudi Padwa, Maharashtra Day (May 1), and Dr. B.R. Ambedkar Jayanti (April 14). Delhi follows central government holidays. Bangalore (Karnataka) observes Kannada Rajyotsava (November 1). Chennai (Tamil Nadu) has Tamil New Year and Pongal. Hyderabad observes Telangana Formation Day.

Second and Fourth Saturday Bank Holidays

Indian banks are closed on the 2nd and 4th Saturdays of every month, all Sundays, and all gazetted bank holidays. This policy was introduced by the RBI and applies to all scheduled commercial banks. Some private sector banks may have slightly different arrangements.
